Cavalcade

h4>

By Kai Bjork

Presence lost and loathing if you were to peel back the bark and lace your tongue in emerald. In the distance she sings Bjork. A sweet refrain in the dark remaining accented by your illuminated moon-corpse creaking in the wind processing through the night like a ghost still with the spirit to haunt the world of the living and it’s flowing, mutable, topographies. It’s not meant to be a strife but pummeling creatures keep pummeling and the earth keeps orbiting the sun; and the moon the earth. The earth you died on and the moon which holds you in its blue glow.
