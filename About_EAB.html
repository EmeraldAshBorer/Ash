About Emerald ash borer

"Emerald Ash Borer, back" by Sam Droege is marked with Public Domain Mark 1.0 .

The Emerald Ash Borer (Agrilus planipennis) is one of the many invasive species that has taken hold in north America. A jewel beetle originating from north-east Asia it has quickly decimated the ash tree population ever since its arrival in Michigan during the 1990s[1]. The decimation of ash has effected our landscape drastically with almost all the ash that gets infested dying off. It has also effected the social and cultural ties we have to this tree, from baseball bats[2] to indigenous basket making[3]. Here in Vermont ash splint basket making has been practiced by the Abanaki for generations—a practice that relies on black ash which is the ash tree most endangered by EAB. There are methods being attempted to mitigate the damage of EAB such as using adaptive silviculture strategies[4] or treating the trees with pesticides such as Emamectin benzoate. Biological control using introduced parisatoid wasps from north east Asia is also employed to control EAB populations[5].
