Erysichthon

By Kai Bjork

Bold as bleeding prayer sucked
out of the shining air we warped.
So boastfully we sung and splintered
a dying lattice of wreaths.

Its spirit is withering and split
at the seams. Lets cannibalize it
tear out its flesh and beg for more
its heart, and soil, and bark, and song

its ripping down the weave
and out through its own insides
it swarms relentlessly and dies
incandescent with eyes cast down

on a sinner of flayed slabs of meat
of real live growing bodies
consumption for consumption
beyond the essence being.

First the burials of the dead for the feast
then the hair and nails and soon the
hand itself. Intestines and thigh
no wriggling away from hunger

its taste punctured on the clay and
the dirt and the branching snag of antlers
roots and dying crowns of ash.

the inside out.
