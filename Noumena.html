Noumena

By Kai Bjork

The rocks are flowing like waves
on the celestial body;

undo the crust age.
Where ferrous deposits

form and endure
until abruptly pickaxed away.

The human sigils
and can’t help but pray

for seasonal fete and joy
under a bouldering

and orange moon
a totality of sky

with lanterns twined up
on either side.

It’s painful to watch
you withering.

It’s painful to dwell
in absence.

We’re here to plant
gardens together

and view the wounds
in solidarity and hope

in the emergence of time
we can always remember

the moon is a real
actual rock

that moves in circles
around this pearl.
